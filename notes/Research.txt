Paper 1: Predicting Student Dropout in Higher Education (Aulck et al.)

https://arxiv.org/pdf/1606.06364

Problem:
Student dropout is a major issue in higher education. Around 30% of first-year students do not return for their second year.

Predictors:
• GPA in math, English, chemistry and psychology courses
• year of enrollment
• birth year
• first term enrollment characteristics

Methods:
Logistic Regression, Random Forest, k-NN

Results:
Logistic Regression achieved ~66% accuracy and AUC ≈ 0.73.

Key Insight:
Early academic performance provides strong signals for predicting student dropout.

Paper 2: University Dropout Prediction through Educational Data Mining Techniques: A Systematic Review

https://je-lks.org/ojs/index.php/Je-LKS_EN/article/view/1135017/1142

Problem: Student dropout rates in higher education range between 30% and 50% in Europe, making student retention an important challenge for universities.

Definition:
Dropout is defined as leaving a university program before completing the degree.

Methods:
Educational Data Mining techniques such as Decision Trees, Neural Networks, Logistic Regression, SVM and k-NN are commonly used.

Key Insight:
Machine learning techniques are widely used to identify students at risk and support early intervention strategies.

Paper 3: Early Prediction of Student Success: Mining Students Enrolment Data

https://repository.openpolytechnic.ac.nz/server/api/core/bitstreams/90765bf0-20a8-41e6-80db-0fa0c42da411/content

Problem:
Predicting student success or failure using enrolment data.

Data:
Student demographic and enrolment variables such as age, gender, ethnicity, work status, and course program.

Methods:
Classification tree models (CHAID and CART).

Results:
The most important predictors were ethnicity, course program, and course block. The models achieved about 60% prediction accuracy.

Key Insight:
Early enrolment data alone has limited predictive power. Additional academic performance variables could improve prediction accuracy.

Paper 4: Early Prediction of Student Dropout in Higher Education using Machine Learning Models ( 2024 )

https://files.eric.ed.gov/fulltext/ED675569.pdf

Problem:
Student dropout in higher education and early identification of at-risk students.

Methods:
Machine learning models including XGBoost and Neural Networks.

Data:
Student demographic data, learning management system activity, and academic achievements.

Results:
Prediction accuracy improves as more academic data becomes available, but early prediction remains important for timely intervention.

Key Insight:
Early prediction using pre-entry and behavioral data can help universities identify students at risk and provide support.
