Questions to explore with the Stakeholder:
	1. Do you think student dropout risk is an important problem in higher education?
	2. Do you think a system like this could be useful in real life, or would it be hard to use in practice?
	3. At what moment do you think this kind of prediction would be most useful: before the semester starts, after the first semester, or later? ( My current project is mostly focused on data after the first semester, but I would like to know what makes the most sense in practice. )
	4. What kind of action could be taken if a student is marked as higher risk?
	5. Do you think it is better for the output to be simple, like dropout/graduate, or as a percentage showing the risk level?
	6. In your opinion, what would make this project successful?
	7. Is it more important to find as many at-risk students as possible, even if some are marked by mistake, or should the model be more careful?
	8. How do you think people should use their own judgment together with the model’s prediction?
	9. Are there any student data fields that you think should not be used, even if they improve the model?
	10. Is there anything important that you think is missing from the idea?
